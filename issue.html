From our Editor in Chief

The Global Ink Literary began from my own discomfort. I started noticing how easily narratives shape young people’s thinking, especially in a time when political opinions circulate faster than understanding. I saw how often young people are able to form strong beliefs based on headlines, short clips, or simplified explanations without fully understandings the systems behind them. At some point, that felt harmful to me especially because of the idea that youth now will eventually be responsible for shaping these very systems. The Global Ink literary was created it because we believe writing should require depth. It should require research, context, and a willingness to question assumptions, including our own. If we expect to participate meaningfully in conversations about global politics, economics, culture, or science, then we have a responsibility to understand what we are speaking about. Through this Issue, "The World in Motion: Stories of Change and Progress," I hope readers see that youth perspectives are strongest when they are informed instead of impulsive. We are not experts (of course), but we have the capability to encourage others to explore different topics and stay informed on our fast paced world!

Our Mission

Our mission is to provide young writers with a platform to be informed on global events rather than simply reacting to them. In a time when opinions form quickly and information spreads rapidly, we believe thoughtful writing requires research, context, and critical analysis. The Global Ink literary exists to encourage literature by publising essay, papers, and various creative works that showcase this willingness to question dominent narratives. Our goal is not to promote a single perspective, but to create a space for informed and well-reasoned ones. By doing so, we hope to help young people strengthen their understanding of the world and contribute meaningfully to the conversations shaping it!
