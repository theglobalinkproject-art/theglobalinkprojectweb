From our Editor in Chief

Global Ink began from my own discomfort. I started noticing how easily narratives shape young people’s thinking, especially in a time when political opinions circulate faster than understanding. Too often, strong beliefs are formed from headlines or short clips without examining the systems behind them, and that felt harmful, particularly knowing that this generation will eventually help shape those systems. I created The Global Ink Literary because I believe writing should require depth, research, and a willingness to question assumptions, including our own. If we want to engage meaningfully in conversations about politics, economics, culture, or science, we have a responsibility to understand what we are speaking about. Through this issue, I hope readers see that youth perspectives are strongest when they are informed rather than impulsive. We may not be experts, but we are capable of encouraging others to stay informed.

Our Mission

Our mission is to provide young writers with a platform to be informed on global events rather than simply reacting to them. In a time when opinions form quickly and information spreads rapidly, we believe thoughtful writing requires research, context, and critical analysis. The Global Ink literary exists to encourage literature by publising essay, papers, and various creative works that showcase this willingness to question dominent narratives. Our goal is not to promote a single perspective, but to create a space for informed and well-reasoned ones. By doing so, we hope to help young people strengthen their understanding of the world and contribute meaningfully to the conversations shaping it!
