Submission Requirements

Note: Please read carefully, as all information provided is important for your submission.

Disclaimer: Professional writing experience is not required. If you've written a piece with effort and passion, we value it and will give it our full attention. Submission does not guarantee acceptance; the editorial team will thoughtfully select pieces for this issue. Once submitted, your work will be carefully reviewed, and if selected, we will contact you within 7 weeks of initial submission.

Issue Theme: “The World in Motion: Stories of Change and Progress”

  • Submissions should explore current global events with a focus on change, transformation, or progress.
  • Pieces should provide context, insight, and fresh perspectives for readers worldwide.

Accepted Submission Types

  • Poetry (300-600 words): Creative poems in any form that explore current global events and highlight transformation, progress, or change.
  • Research Papers (1300–2500 words): Structured analyses of recent global events, using evidence to explain causes, context, and broader significance.
  • Review Articles (1300–2500 words): Critical summaries of existing research or reporting that identify trends, debates, and insights on global events.
  • Viewpoint / Opinion Articles (800–1500 words): Persuasive essays presenting well-reasoned perspectives on current events or developments.
  • Short Reports (500–1000 words): Clear, factual summaries of recent global events or trends, emphasizing key outcomes and implications.
  • Essays & Personal Narratives (800–1500 words): Reflective or analytical works connecting personal observations to larger global events or changes.
  • Event Analyses (1000–2000 words): Detailed breakdowns of recent global events, examining causes, developments, and broader impact.

Formatting & Requirements

  • PDF only; 12-point Times New Roman; double-spaced (poetry may be single-spaced)
  • Include full name, submission date, title, and page numbers
  • One submission per author; must follow word count rules
  • Original, unpublished work only; no plagiarism or AI-generated content

Editorial Focus

  • Highlight relevant global events, change, or progress
  • Offer unique perspectives and critical thinking
  • Ensure clarity, accuracy, and credible sources
  • Engage and inform readers while reflecting the issue theme

Submission Process

  • Submit via our official submission form (click on Submit Your Work button on the top of this page)
  • No submission fee
  • Selected pieces will be published in The Global Ink Literary, Issue 1
